1. Reddit value reply

link-freelowest-risk first movebirth-time uncertainty
One thing that gets missed in chart-based readings is that the birth record is part of the interpretation, not just a data entry step.

Korean Saju / Four Pillars is a useful comparison because it does not use houses, planets, signs, or aspects. It starts from year, month, day, and hour pillars, then reads stems, branches, five elements, seasonal balance, and timing flow.

For global birth records, I would want the time basis checked before saying anything confident:

- recorded local civil birth time
- birth city and country
- historical timezone for that date
- daylight saving time
- whether true solar time could matter near an hour boundary
- whether the time is exact, rounded, remembered, or only approximate

That does not make the reading more certain. It makes the uncertainty visible. If the birth record is fuzzy, the reading should say what was checked and what remains uncertain.
