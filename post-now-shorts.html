One thing I find interesting when comparing Western astrology with Korean Saju/Four Pillars is that the reading depends a lot on the time basis before interpretation even starts.

Saju does not use houses, planets, signs, MC/IC, or aspects. It starts from the year, month, day, and hour pillars, then reads the relationship between stems, branches, five elements, seasonal balance, and timing cycles.

For someone born outside Korea, I would want the reader to check recorded local birth time, birth city, historical timezone, daylight saving time, and whether true solar time could affect the hour block.

That is the part English explanations often miss: the useful question is not only "what does this pillar mean?" but "was the time basis handled honestly before the reading started?"
